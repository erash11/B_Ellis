# Force Plate Training Report - Design Specification

**Based on conversation with colleague - November 20, 2024**

---

## Report Philosophy

This report translates force plate metrics into **actionable training recommendations** without prescribing specific exercises. The goal is to:

1. **Categorize athletes** based on force plate trends (Categories 1-7 primary)
2. **Suggest training modalities** (execution styles, not exercises)
3. **Guide periodization decisions** at 4-6 week phase transitions
4. **Respect coach autonomy** - suggest, don't mandate

Key principle: "We're not recreating anything new we haven't already done. We're just dipping back into things we already do, and it's leading us into when and why to use those techniques."

## Category Definitions & Recommendations

### CATEGORY 1: Maximal Strength Capacity
**Metrics Monitored:**
- IMTP Peak Force â†“
- Net Peak Vertical Force â†“

**WEIGHT ROOM SUGGESTIONS:**
- Heavy isometrics with time-under-tension (TUT) emphasis
- Cluster sets (â‰¥80% 1RM)
- Wave loading protocols
- Longer rest intervals between sets (4-5 min)

**FIELD SUGGESTIONS:**
- Resisted sprints (50-60% velocity decrement)
- Heavy sled pushes

**Execution Notes:**
"Still do your planned exercises (trap bar, safety bar, whatever), but execute them with longer rest, cluster structure, or add isometric holds. Not changing exercises, just changing how we perform them."

---

### CATEGORY 2: Explosive Strength / Rate of Force Development (RFD)
**Metrics Monitored:**
- IMTP Force @ 50ms â†“
- IMTP Force @ 100ms â†“
- IMTP Force @ 200ms â†“

**WEIGHT ROOM SUGGESTIONS:**
- Contrast/complex training (heavy lift â†’ explosive movement)
- Olympic lift variations (60-70% 1RM)
- Accommodating resistance (bands/chains) with fast concentric intent
- Cluster sets with explosive emphasis

**FIELD SUGGESTIONS:**
- Contrast/complex training on field
- Resisted sprints (10-30% velocity decrement)

**Execution Notes:**
"Example: If you planned trap bar deadlifts, pair them with vertical jumps in a superset. Or do jammer press into plyometric push-ups. Still the same movements you planned, just executed as contrasts."

---

### CATEGORY 3: Power Output / Force-Velocity Expression
**Metrics Monitored:**
- Peak Power (CMJ or IMTP) â†“

**WEIGHT ROOM SUGGESTIONS:**
- Contrast/complex training
- Cluster sets (70-80% 1RM)
- Olympic lift variations
- Ballistic movements emphasis

**FIELD SUGGESTIONS:**
- Contrast/complex training
- Resisted sprints (30-50% velocity decrement)

**Execution Notes:**
"Focus on moving submaximal loads with maximal intent. If you're doing power cleans, use cluster sets. If you're doing squats, follow with jump squats."

---

### CATEGORY 4: Stretch-Shortening Cycle (SSC) Efficiency
**Metrics Monitored:**
- RSI-mod â†“
- Eccentric Braking RFD â†“

**WEIGHT ROOM SUGGESTIONS:**
- Reactive/ballistic techniques
- Drop jumps, altitude landings
- Reactive medicine ball work

**FIELD SUGGESTIONS:**
- Plyometric training (pogos, bounds, hurdle hops)
- Reactive agility drills

**Execution Notes:**
"Emphasize quick ground contacts. When doing plyos, focus on minimal ground contact time rather than max height. Think 'hot ground' mentality."

---

### CATEGORY 5: Eccentric Control & Braking Strength
**Metrics Monitored:**
- Eccentric Mean Braking Force â†“
- Eccentric RFD â†“

**WEIGHT ROOM SUGGESTIONS:**
- Eccentrics with TUT (3-5 second down phases)
- Accentuated eccentric overload (105-120% 1RM)
- Tempo squats/RDLs (slow eccentric focus)
- Reverse band work (overload eccentric, deload concentric)

**FIELD SUGGESTIONS:**
- Change of direction drills
- Deceleration-focused work

**Execution Notes:**
"Example: If doing safety bar squats, add a 5-count down. Or use reverse bands where they help on the way up but you control heavy weight on the way down. We've done this before with reverse band squat and bench."

---

### CATEGORY 6: Technical / Coordination Efficiency
**Metrics Monitored:**
- Contraction Time â†‘
- Time to Peak Force â†‘
- Eccentric RFD changes

**WEIGHT ROOM SUGGESTIONS:**
- Wave loading (varying loads within session)
- Cluster sets
- Rhythmic tempo work (consistent cadence)
- Motor control focus

**FIELD SUGGESTIONS:**
- Movement skill retraining
- Motor control drills
- Technical sprint work (not conditioning)

**Execution Notes:**
"Think movement quality over load. If technique is degrading, reduce intensity and focus on smooth execution. Use wave loading to get quality reps at different intensities."

---

### CATEGORY 7: Asymmetry / Limb Imbalance
**Metrics Monitored:**
- L-R Force Asymmetry > 10%
- L-R Impulse or Power differences

**WEIGHT ROOM SUGGESTIONS:**
- Single-leg/unilateral strength work
- Split-stance patterns (split squats, single-leg RDLs)
- Unilateral jumps and landings
- Offset loading

**FIELD SUGGESTIONS:**
- Unilateral jumps
- Single-leg bounds
- Asymmetrical agility work

**Execution Notes:**
"If squats are programmed, replace with split squats. If doing power cleans, add single-leg box jumps as accessory. Address the imbalance through exercise selection."

---

### CATEGORY 8: Fatigue / Movement Strategy Shift (SECONDARY)
**Metrics Monitored:**
- Contraction Time â†‘ + Jump Height â†“
- OR Contraction Time â†‘ + Peak Power â†“

**WEIGHT ROOM SUGGESTIONS:**
- Reduce total tonnage 10-20% (one level down)
- Increase rest intervals
- Add mobility/movement prep
- Skill patterning work (low intensity, high quality)

**FIELD SUGGESTIONS:**
- Active recovery sessions
- Lower volume/intensity field work

**Execution Notes:**
"This is a 'tap the brakes' category. Don't change exercises, just reduce volume or intensity by 1 level. If doing 4 sets, drop to 3. If doing 5 reps, drop to 4."

---

### CATEGORY 9: Systemic Fatigue State (SECONDARY)
**Metrics Monitored:**
- RSI-mod â†“ + Peak Power â†“ (both > 10%)

**WEIGHT ROOM SUGGESTIONS:**
- Deload block (reduce load 15-25%, drop 2 levels)
- Active recovery emphasis
- Restoration micro-cycle
- Technical work only

**FIELD SUGGESTIONS:**
- Restoration work
- Active recovery only
- No conditioning

**Execution Notes:**

## Report Usage Guidelines

### For Coaches

**Timing:**
- Receive report in the **last week of current phase**
- Use to inform **next phase planning** (which is likely already drafted)
- Make **incremental adjustments**, not wholesale rewrites

**Interpretation:**
- **If many athletes are RED** â†’ Prioritize multiple interventions from that category
- **If athletes are YELLOW/ORANGE** â†’ Select 1-2 primary interventions
- **If category is heavily populated** â†’ This should guide phase emphasis
- **If multiple categories flagged** â†’ Start with highest severity, then address others

**Decision Making:**
- The report **suggests**, you **decide**
- Use your coaching judgment and context the data can't see
- Consider: injury history, position demands, competition schedule, mental state

**Making Changes:**
You DON'T need to:
- âŒ Change all exercises
- âŒ Rewrite entire program
- âŒ Add new movements you've never done

You DO need to:
- âœ… Adjust execution style (tempo, rest, pairing)
- âœ… Consider sequencing (superset vs. separate)
- âœ… Modify loading schemes (clusters, wave loading)
- âœ… Keep exercises but change how they're performed
